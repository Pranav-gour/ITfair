HISTORY OF QUANTUM COMPUTERS

THE TIMELINE OF QUANTUM COMPUTERS

  • First Theories

    The history of quantum computers started when the idea of building quantum computers which was theorized by Pual Benioff was proposed by Richard Feynman and Yuri Manin in the early 1980s.

  • Father of Quantum Computing

    The father of Quantum Computing, David Deutsch, first decribed a universal computer based on quantum physics.

  • First Quantum Algorithm

    Peter Shor, a mathematician at MIT, dicorved the first quantum algorithm to find a number's prime factors.

  • First Quantum Computer

    In 1998, the first ever working 2-Qubit Quantum Computer was launched and demonstrated at The Oxford University.

  • The 1st Qubyte

    The first quantum byte (a unit of 8 Qubits) was created by the scientists of Innsbruck in Austria.

  • Quantum Computers developed by a company

    In 2017, IBM succesfully tests a 16-Qubit computer, also, in the same year, Harvard announced a 51-Qubit quantum computer.
